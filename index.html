Food Recall Enforcement

Food recalls are safety actions used to remove contaminated or incorrectly labeled products from the market. This story looks at why recalls happen, how serious they are, and how long they remain active.

1. Background

There is a rigorous process of classifying danger, notifying the public, and removing products from store shelves. The process only officially ends once the FDA has verified that all contaminated items are accounted for, a logistical cycle that can often take over a year to complete.

2. Leading Causes of FDA Food Recalls

Key Point

The two largest causes of food recalls are listeria and undeclared allergens. These are both highly preventable but also highly dangerous to affected individuals.

3. Recalls by Year and Severity

Key Point

A majority of food recalls are Class I (44.2%) and Class II (50.0%), while only 5.8% are Class III. Most recalls are therefore tied to meaningful health risk.

4. How Long Food Recalls Remain Active

Key Point

Food recalls are not resolved immediately. There can be a significant amount of time between when a recall is announced and when it is closed, and this delay appears across severity classes.

5. Takeaway

y

Food recalls are caused mainly by issues that can be easily preventable, but most are classified as cases of high severity. These recalls can also take a long time to get resolved, which might pose a risk for people still consuming the products.
